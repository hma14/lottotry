About the New York Lottery

The state of New York (NY) was one of the pioneers of government-run lotteries in the U.S and was the third state lottery to be opened back in 1967. Over the years, the various New York lottery draws have created revenue that has been used primarily in the education sector, as well as developing manufacturing industries and general state and city maintenance.

There are currently eight lotteries in New York: New York Lotto, Midday Numbers, Midday Win 4, Numbers, Take 5, Win 4, Pick 10, Sweet Million, as well as the larger multi-state lotteries: Mega Millions and Powerball. Of these, the New York Lotto has been the most popular and is also one of biggest selling U.S lotteries, with millions of people checking the results of the draw every week.

Of the New York lotteries, the New York Lotto and Sweet Million are generally the lotteries with the biggest jackpots, with a full set of winning numbers often rewarding the ticket holder with tens of millions of dollars.
